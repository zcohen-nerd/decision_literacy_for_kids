Welcome to **Decision Literacy for Kids** — an 18-week curriculum that teaches young learners (ages 7-11) to think clearly about uncertainty, recognize their brain's default settings, and systematically improve how they make choices.

<!-- truncate -->

## What This Curriculum Covers

Over 18 weeks (plus 2 optional extension weeks), students explore five big themes:

1. **Probability & The Physics of Choice** (Weeks 1-4) — Randomness, probability, process vs. outcome thinking, and building the Decision Journal.
2. **Debugging the Hardware** (Weeks 5-8) — Cognitive biases, loss aversion, sunk costs, framing effects, and learning to spot them in the wild.
3. **Data & Signal Processing** (Weeks 9-11) — Expected value, signal vs. noise, and matching decision effort to stakes.
4. **Game Theory & Social Systems** (Weeks 12-14) — How your choices affect others, the Prisoner's Dilemma, and the Tragedy of the Commons.
5. **The Optimization Project** (Weeks 15-18) — Apply everything to a real personal challenge: identify, design, test, and iterate.

## Who It's For

The curriculum is designed for **caregivers, educators, and homeschool families** working with kids aged roughly 7-11. Each week includes two guided sessions (~30 minutes each) led by an adult and one independent session for the student to practice on their own.
