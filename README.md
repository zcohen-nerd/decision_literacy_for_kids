# About the Curriculum

This project provides an **18-week introduction to decision literacy** designed for young learners (roughly ages 7-11, with adult guidance as needed).

Instead of teaching kids *what* to decide, the curriculum teaches them *how* to decide — building **five core mental models** that form the foundation of good decision-making:

1. **Probabilistic Outcomes** — The world runs on likelihoods, not certainties
2. **Brain Shortcuts (Heuristics)** — Your brain takes shortcuts that sometimes mislead
3. **Opportunity Cost** — Every choice has a hidden price tag
4. **Diminishing Returns** — More isn't always better
5. **Iterative Decisions** — Design → Test → Measure → Patch → Repeat

The central throughline is the **Decision Journal** — a running record where students document their reasoning *before* seeing the result, creating a powerful tool for honest self-reflection.

# Curriculum Structure

The curriculum progresses through five phases:

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|------|------|------|
| Probability & The Physics of Choice | 1–4 | Randomness, probability, process vs. outcome, the Decision Journal |
| Debugging the Hardware | 5–8 | Heuristics, loss aversion, sunk costs, framing, bias hunting |
| Data & Signal Processing | 9–11 | Expected value, signal vs. noise, reversible vs. irreversible decisions |
| Game Theory & Social Systems | 12–14 | Ripple effects, Prisoner's Dilemma, Tragedy of the Commons |
| The Optimisation Project | 15–18 | Identify a real problem, design a protocol, test it, iterate |
| Advanced Topics (Optional) | Extension | Bayesian updating, decision trees & multi-step strategy |

Each week includes:

- **Two guided sessions** (caregiver-led, ~30 min each)
- **One independent session** (student-led practice and Decision Journal entry)

The curriculum emphasises **exploration, reasoning, hands-on activities, and real-world application**.

# Why This Curriculum Exists

Many children grow up making thousands of decisions but never learn how to think through them carefully. They inherit gut instincts and cultural defaults without understanding why they work (or when they don't).

This curriculum aims to change that.

The goal is simple:

**Give kids a toolkit for understanding uncertainty, recognising their own cognitive biases, and systematically improving how they make choices** — so that by the time they face the big decisions in life, they already know how to think clearly under pressure.
